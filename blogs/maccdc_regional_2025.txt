17:T1a7f,<h1>🌸 MACCDC 2025: Securing Networks and Sniffing Flowers in DC 🌼</h1>
<p>Well well well, if it isn't the coziest cyber tale of the year! 💻🐾 On <strong>April 21st and 22nd, 2025</strong>, our team packed our laptops, snacks, and lots of monster, and set out on a big adventure to MACCDC — a <strong>blue teaming cybersecurity competition</strong> where we had to <em>defend a live network</em> from an active red team trying to sneak in and cause chaos. 😤💥</p>
<p>Spoiler alert: we had an <em>amazing</em> time.</p>
<p> </p>
<h2>🌿 What Even <em>Is</em> MACCDC?</h2>
<p>MACCDC (Mid-Atlantic Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition, but whew, that’s a mouthful!) is a yearly competition where teams take on the role of a company’s IT department. In 2025, we had to <strong>secure a pre-built network</strong> under pressure while a red team tried everything they could to break in. The whole thing lasted <em>two full days</em>:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Day 1</strong> was all about the <em>on-premises topology</em> (servers, firewalls, oh my!).</li>
<li><strong>Day 2</strong> brought in the <em>cloudy chaos</em> — Amazon Web Services (AWS) and everything that comes with it.</li>
</ul>
<p>And through it all? We patched, monitored, splunked, reconfigured, and maybe cried a little bit.</p>
<p> </p>
<h2>🐾 Meet Our Cozy Little Cyber Squad</h2>
<p>Like villagers working together to keep the island running, each teammate had a special role, and we couldn’t have done it without everyone!</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Harris Ransom</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Splunk 9.1.1</strong> and <strong>Cisco</strong><br>
Oh Harris… where do we begin? If you were within five feet of him at any point, you probably heard the word <em>Splunk</em>. Over and over. And over. It went a little something like this:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“I’m in Splunk. Splunk’s not working. Splunk won’t load. Splunk is Splunking. Splunk is broken. Splunk. Splunk. Splunk.”</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Despite the struggle (and oh boy, it was real), he pushed through and wrangled those logs like a champ. You did good, Splunker.</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Mohamad Khawam</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Cisco</strong> and <strong>Ubuntu Workstation</strong><br>
Mohamad was one of the calmest beans in the bunch. No matter what chaos erupted around him (and trust, there was a lot), he quietly and methodically solved issues. If MACCDC were a museum, Mohamad would be the security guard gently shooing intruders away with a smile. 🚦🐢</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Keeret Saggu</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>CentOS ecommerce</strong>, <strong>Windows 10</strong>, and <strong>internal pentesting</strong><br>
Keeret wasn’t just defending — he was <em>attacking</em> our own network to help find vulnerabilities <em>before</em> the red team could exploit them. Smart cookie 🍪!</p>
<p>He also drove... sort of. He had his Tesla charging at a station, but the catch? It was <em>charging for five hours every five minutes</em>. Meanwhile, he was gaming on the built-in console like a true 3020s digital nomad. We still don't know how he pulled it off.</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Rhea Sharma</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Active Directory 2019</strong>, <strong>DNS</strong>, and <strong>DHCP</strong><br>
Rhea was the backbone of identity services. Seriously, if users couldn’t log in or DNS got scrambled, she was already on it before anyone even noticed. Calm, cool, collected — a real island mayor if you ask us. 👑🌐</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Arnav Kumar</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Fedora 21 SMTP</strong>, <strong>Website</strong>, <strong>Ubuntu AWS</strong>, <strong>Docker</strong>, and <strong>Webmail</strong><br>
Yes, you read that right. All of that. Arnav basically turned into a living multi-tool. Like, imagine trying to fish, catch bugs, terraform your island, and craft fences all at the same time. That was Arnav. 🛠️💌🐛
He was also late everyday. Like...every single day. He was the last one to show up to our team meetings. <em>shakes head</em></p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Dhanush Vajravelu</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Cisco</strong>, <strong>DNS</strong>, and <strong>NTP</strong><br>
The network timing and routing maestro! Also, our literal driver — though his car was packed with <strong>AI Jschlatt</strong> and <strong>Yuno Miles</strong> blasting through the aux like we were cruising through the digital wilderness. Let’s just say it was a <em>vibe</em>. 🎶🚗🔥</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Oleg Gayduk</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Ubuntu Website</strong><br>
Oleg quietly kept our website afloat like a lone lighthouse in a stormy sea. If the website went down, we would’ve had some sad villagers on our hands. He kept those pages loading smooth like honey. 🍯🖥️</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Ryan Petit</h3>
<p>Handled: <strong>Docker</strong> and <strong>Remote Windows 2019</strong><br>
Ryan was all about those containers and remote systems. He was supposed to drive too, but... well...</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“Engine go kaput.”</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Poor guy. But he still showed up strong on the tech side. We salute your dedication, Ryan. 🫡🚙💨</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Amelia (Me! 🍄)</h3>
<p>Role: <strong>Alternate / CTF Explorer</strong><br>
I wasn’t part of the competition squad itself, but while they were fighting the red team, I dove into a fun little <strong>HackTheBox CTF</strong> on Day 1 and <em>found a wooden cube</em>. What does it mean?? Who knows! But it was cube-shaped. And wooden. Definitely an experience. 🎁🌲</p>
<p> </p>
<h3>🌟 Patrick Bubniak</h3>
<p>Role: <strong>Alternate</strong><br>
Patrick was also a fun member on the team, helping to keep everyone motivated and on task through the nightmare cascade that was CCDC! Sometimes being a good teammate just means making sure people eat and sleep (or at least <em>try</em> to sleep). 💤💙</p>
<p> </p>
<h2>🎉 Post-Comp Celebration in Washington DC</h2>
<p>Once all the firewalls were fortified, logs parsed, and DNS zones secured, we <em>celebrated</em>! We roamed around <strong>Washington DC</strong>, exploring the city like curious little villagers visiting the big city. Museums, monuments, food, laughs — a perfect way to end such a high-stakes weekend. 🏛️🌸🍽️</p>
<p> </p>
<h2>🌼 Final Thoughts</h2>
<p>MACCDC 2025 was wild, challenging, and so full of learning. Our team faced every obstacle head-on, and whether it was Splunk being a rascal or Tesla charger déjà vu, we kept our spirits high.</p>
<p>Would we do it all over again? Absolutely. But maybe with fewer charging breaks and more snacks.</p>
